# Hubble

Edwin Hubble (1889–1953). His big moves: proved in 1925 that [[M31
Andromeda]] is a separate galaxy (using [[Cepheid variable|Cepheids]]
to measure its distance), and in 1929 published the linear relationship
between galactic distance and [[Redshift|recession velocity]] that
now carries his name.

Hubble didn't originate either idea alone. Henrietta [[Leavitt]]
built the tool he used. Georges Lemaître derived the expansion
prediction from general relativity two years before Hubble's paper.
Still, Hubble was the person at the 100-inch Mount Wilson telescope
getting the data.

The space telescope is named after him; appropriate.

Related: [[Black hole]], [[Parallax]].
