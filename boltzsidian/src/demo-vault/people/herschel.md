# Herschel

William Herschel (1738–1822), a musician turned astronomer who built
his own telescopes — bigger than any professional observatory's at
the time. His 40-foot scope had a 48-inch mirror, which held the
aperture record for fifty years.

He discovered Uranus (1781) by accident — first thought it was a
comet. Catalogued thousands of nebulae and double stars with his
sister Caroline, who found eight comets of her own and never got
quite enough credit.

Herschel also discovered infrared radiation in 1800 by sticking
thermometers past the red end of a solar spectrum. The first
non-visible light ever detected, found with a prism and patience.
