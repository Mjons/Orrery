# Leavitt

Henrietta Swan Leavitt (1868–1921). One of the Harvard "computers" —
the group of women who processed the observatory's photographic
plates on a flat rate of twenty-five cents an hour.

From a stack of plates of the Small Magellanic Cloud she noticed in
1908 that the brightest [[Cepheid variable|Cepheid variables]] had the
longest periods. Because all SMC stars are effectively the same
distance from us, the period-luminosity relation was immediate and
unambiguous — and it gave astronomy the first standard candle with
genuine reach.

[[Hubble]] used Leavitt's relation to prove that [[M31 Andromeda]] is
a separate galaxy. Every distance measurement in cosmology since,
including [[Redshift|Hubble's law]] and the expanding universe, stands
on her rung of the ladder.

Related: [[Parallax]].
