# Messier

Charles Messier (1730–1817) catalogued 110 non-cometary nebulosities
because he kept confusing them with comets, which were the celebrity
objects of his day and the focus of his career.

The Messier catalog is an accidental greatest-hits list for amateur
astronomy. [[M31 Andromeda]], [[M42 Orion Nebula]], [[M13 Hercules
Cluster]], [[M45 Pleiades]], [[M51 Whirlpool Galaxy]], [[M57 Ring
Nebula]] — the objects most amateur scopes will pull out of a
suburban sky.

Messier himself never saw his catalog the way we now use it. He'd be
amused by the annual "Messier Marathon" — chasing all 110 in a single
night, only possible in late March when the Sun is at the right RA.
