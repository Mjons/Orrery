# Galileo

Galileo Galilei (1564–1642) didn't invent the telescope but he was
the first to point a good one at the sky and write down what he
saw. _Sidereus Nuncius_ (1610) was less than a hundred pages long
and it changed everything.

What he reported: the mountainous Moon. Four satellites of Jupiter.
The phases of Venus. Countless stars invisible to the naked eye.
The phases of Venus in particular made Ptolemy's geocentric model
untenable — Venus had to orbit the Sun, not the Earth.

He got nearly everything right except Saturn's rings, which he
recorded as "ears" because his scope wasn't quite good enough to
resolve them.
