# Cepheid variable

A pulsating yellow supergiant whose brightness varies on a regular
period (1–100 days) tied to its luminosity. Because the relationship
is predictable, measuring a Cepheid's period gives its absolute
magnitude, and comparing to apparent magnitude gives its distance.

[[Leavitt]] found the period-luminosity relation in 1908 from variables
in the Small Magellanic Cloud, a galaxy where all the stars are
effectively the same distance from us — which meant comparing their
apparent brightnesses was equivalent to comparing their intrinsic
brightnesses.

[[Hubble]] used Cepheids in [[M31 Andromeda]] to prove it was its own
galaxy. They remain the second rung of the cosmic distance ladder
above [[Parallax]].

[[Polaris]] is a Cepheid, though the prototype of the class is Delta
Cephei itself.

Related: [[Redshift]].
