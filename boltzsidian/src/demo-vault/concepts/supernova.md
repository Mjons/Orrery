# Supernova

A star's abrupt death, bright enough to outshine its entire host galaxy
for weeks. Two main flavours:

**Core-collapse (Type II / Ib / Ic).** A massive star's iron core
collapses in under a second; the infall bounces off the proto-neutron
star and tears the outer layers apart. [[Betelgeuse]], [[Rigel]],
[[Deneb]] are all candidates, on wildly different timescales.

**Thermonuclear (Type Ia).** A white dwarf in a binary accretes past
the Chandrasekhar limit (~1.44 M☉) and detonates completely. Remarkably
uniform peak luminosity, which is what makes Ia's the standard candle
used to measure [[Redshift|cosmological distances]].

Historic nearby events: SN 1054 (the Crab pulsar's birth), Tycho's 1572,
Kepler's 1604. I was eight years old for SN 1987A. Nothing visible in
our galaxy since.

Related: [[Black hole]] (another possible core-collapse outcome).
