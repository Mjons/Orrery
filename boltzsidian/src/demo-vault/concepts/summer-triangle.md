# Summer Triangle

Not a constellation — an asterism. Three bright stars spanning three
constellations: [[Vega]] in [[Lyra]], [[Altair]] in [[Aquila]], and
[[Deneb]] in [[Cygnus]].

In the northern hemisphere, the Triangle is overhead through summer
and into early autumn. It's the first thing I find when I go out for
a summer session — easier than picking out any single constellation
from a suburban yard — and everything else reads from there.

Three very different stars: Vega is a nearby A-type main-sequence
star, Altair is a fast-rotating A-type, Deneb is a blue supergiant
many thousand times further away than either. The Triangle is a
geometrical coincidence, not a physical group.
