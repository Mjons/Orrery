# Precession

The Earth spins with an axial tilt of 23.4°, and that axis traces a
slow circle against the fixed stars — one circuit every 25,772 years.
Caused by the gravitational torques of the Moon and Sun on Earth's
equatorial bulge.

The practical consequence: the pole star changes. [[Polaris]] is the
current one. About 5,000 years ago Thuban in Draco held the title.
In 12,000 years [[Vega]] will. The zodiac sign on your birthday is
about a sign off from where the Sun actually is, for the same reason.

Hipparchus noticed precession in ~127 BCE by comparing his star positions
to older records. That comparison is the first known example of
catalog-scale observational astronomy.

Related: [[Parallax]].
