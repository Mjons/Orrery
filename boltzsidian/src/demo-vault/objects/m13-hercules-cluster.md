# M13 Hercules Cluster

The finest globular cluster in northern skies — about 25,000 light-years
away in the constellation Hercules, discovered by Halley in 1714 and
catalogued by [[Messier]] in 1764.

At magnitude 5.8 it's a naked-eye smudge from a truly dark site. From
my yard it's a binocular target; at 150× in the 8" the outer edges
resolve into a sprinkle of tiny stars. Hundreds of thousands of stars
packed into a ball perhaps a hundred light-years across.

M13 was the target of the 1974 Arecibo Message — the first intentional
interstellar radio transmission. It'll arrive in about 25,000 years,
long after the cluster has moved.
