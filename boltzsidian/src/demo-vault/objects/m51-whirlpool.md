# M51 Whirlpool Galaxy

A face-on grand-design spiral being actively merged with its smaller
companion NGC 5195, sitting just off the end of the [[Ursa Major|Big
Dipper]]'s handle. About 23 million light-years away.

M51 was the first galaxy where spiral structure was resolved — Lord
Rosse did it in 1845 with the Leviathan of Parsonstown. That's the
first documented sketch of what we now know is a spiral galaxy, decades
before anyone understood galaxies were separate systems.

From Bortle 6 the spiral arms are invisible to the eye but show up
clearly in a short exposure. Good candidate for the astrophoto project.

Related: [[Hubble]], [[Redshift]].
