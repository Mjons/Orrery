# M31 Andromeda

Our nearest big galactic neighbour — a barred spiral, about 2.5 million
light-years away, moving toward us at 110 km/s. In roughly four and a
half billion years it will collide with the Milky Way; the resulting
merged object is provisionally nicknamed Milkomeda.

Under Bortle 6 I can just barely see the bright central hub with the
naked eye, off the corner of Cassiopeia. In the binos the halo
extends a remarkable distance. In the 8" the structure is muddy —
you'd want a dark sky for real spiral detail.

M31 is the [[Hubble]]-era proof that the Milky Way isn't the only
galaxy: Edwin Hubble's 1925 [[Cepheid variable|Cepheid]] measurements
put it far outside our own galaxy, ending the Great Debate.

Related: [[Redshift]] (weirdly, M31 is blueshifted — it's gravitationally
bound to us).
