# M42 Orion Nebula

The naked-eye emission nebula hanging from [[Orion]]'s sword. A sheet
of gas and dust about 1,300 light-years away, lit from inside by the
hot young stars of the Trapezium cluster.

In the 8" at 80×, the wings sweep out in characteristic bat-shape. With
an OIII filter the structure deepens and you start to see filaments.

M42 is the nearest large star-forming region to us, and probably the
single most-photographed deep-sky object in history. If I had to
recommend one object to a new telescope owner, it would be this.

Part of the Orion Molecular Cloud Complex — so more or less everything
bright in [[Orion]] is a sibling.

Related: [[Photograph globulars]] (different kind of target entirely,
but the filter technique carries over).
