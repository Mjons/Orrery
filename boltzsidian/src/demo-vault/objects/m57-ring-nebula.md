# M57 Ring Nebula

The classic planetary nebula, sitting between the two southern stars
of the [[Lyra]] parallelogram. A small grey smoke-ring about 2,500
light-years away.

Planetary nebulae have nothing to do with planets — the name is an
18th-century visual mistake, from the disc-like appearance in small
scopes. What they actually are: the outer atmospheres of low-mass
stars, puffed off as the star becomes a white dwarf. In ~5 billion
years the Sun will do exactly this.

M57 is small, so it takes magnification. At 250× in the 8" it's a
neat oval with a darker center — the white dwarf itself is at
magnitude 15 and probably out of reach from my yard without stacking.

Related: [[Supernova]] (which is what bigger stars do instead).
