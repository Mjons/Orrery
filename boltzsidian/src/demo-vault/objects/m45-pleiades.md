# M45 Pleiades

The Seven Sisters. An open cluster about 440 light-years away, young
enough (~100 million years) that its brightest stars are still hot
B-types burning blue.

Naked-eye six or seven stars in a dipper-shape; binoculars show dozens;
wide-field imaging picks up the bluish reflection nebula draped over
the cluster as it sails through an unrelated cloud of interstellar dust.

In winter, the Pleiades ride just ahead of [[Orion]] in my setup
routine: I catch the Pleiades at zenith, then drop southeast to [[M42
Orion Nebula]]. A natural sequence.
