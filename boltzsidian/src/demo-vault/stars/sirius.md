# Sirius

The brightest star in the night sky, at apparent magnitude −1.46. It is
about 8.6 light-years away — close enough that you can see its proper
motion shift over a human lifetime, if you are patient and keep good
notes.

Sirius is a binary. The companion, Sirius B, is a white dwarf — a stellar
remnant no larger than Earth but carrying a sun's worth of mass. You can
separate them in a big scope on a still night; [[Herschel]] thought
he glimpsed the B star in 1844 but the confirmation was photographic,
decades later.

Sirius belongs to Canis Major, following [[Orion]] up the winter sky.

Related: [[Parallax]] (its distance was one of the first measured),
[[Vega]] (a near-twin by colour).
