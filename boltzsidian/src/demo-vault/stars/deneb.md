# Deneb

The tail of [[Cygnus]] the swan. Also the northernmost vertex of the
[[Summer Triangle|Summer Triangle]] (the other two being [[Vega]] and
[[Altair]]).

Deneb is further from us than a naked-eye star has any business being —
about 2,600 light-years, give or take the usual Gaia-era revisions. It
burns absurdly bright, between 50,000 and 200,000 solar luminosities. If
you dropped it where Sirius is, it would cast shadows in daylight.

Deneb is a candidate [[Supernova|future supernova]] — not as urgently as
[[Betelgeuse]], but one to keep a distant eye on.
