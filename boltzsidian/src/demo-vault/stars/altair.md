# Altair

The brightest star in [[Aquila]], the eagle. Only about 17 light-years
away — one of our nearest bright neighbours.

Altair rotates freakishly fast — once every nine hours or so, which
squashes it into an oblate spheroid. The CHARA interferometer
resolved its disc in 2006 and showed the shape directly.

Altair is one corner of the [[Summer Triangle|Summer Triangle]] with
[[Vega]] and [[Deneb]]. When I'm setting up the scope in July,
"triangle up, then find the teapot" is how I start.

Related: [[Deep sky challenge]] (all three triangle stars are
step-off points for finding targets).
