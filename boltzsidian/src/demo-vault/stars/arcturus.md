# Arcturus

Fourth-brightest star overall; brightest in the northern celestial
hemisphere. Orange giant, 37 light-years away, sitting at the foot of
the kite-shape of Boötes.

Arcturus is an old star running out of hydrogen in its core — it's moved
off the main sequence onto the red giant branch. It's also an unusual
mover: a "high-velocity" halo star, part of a stream of old stars that
likely came from a dwarf galaxy eaten by the [[M31 Andromeda|Milky Way's
neighbourhood]] eons ago.

I use it as a pointer: "arc to Arcturus, speed on to Spica." From
the handle of the [[Ursa Major|Big Dipper]], follow the curve south
until you hit a bright orange dot.

Related: [[Parallax]].
