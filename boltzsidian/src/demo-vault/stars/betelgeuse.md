# Betelgeuse

The red shoulder of [[Orion]]. A red supergiant, roughly 700 times the
Sun's diameter — large enough that if you plopped it where the Sun is,
its photosphere would eat Jupiter.

Betelgeuse is pulsating and dimming on a ~400-day cycle, and in late 2019
it underwent the "Great Dimming," shedding a chunk of atmosphere that
passed across our line of sight. Amateurs tracking it with DSLRs saw the
brightness drop by more than a magnitude. It's still there.

It will eventually go [[Supernova|supernova]]. Timescale: somewhere between
tomorrow and a hundred thousand years from now — the kind of range that
makes "soon" feel funny.

Related: [[Rigel]] (the other shoulder).
