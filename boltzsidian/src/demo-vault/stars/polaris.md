# Polaris

The current North Star, less than a degree off the celestial pole. It's
a triple system anchored by a Cepheid-variable supergiant — the Cepheid
itself has been slowly losing pulsation amplitude over the last century,
which is slightly unnerving if you grew up using it as an anchor.

Polaris hasn't always been the pole star and won't always be. Because
of [[Precession]] the Earth's axis traces a 26,000-year circle on the
sky — [[Vega]] was the pole star around 12,000 BCE, and it'll be again
by the year 14,000.

Finding it: draw a line from the two outer stars of the [[Ursa Major|Big
Dipper]]'s bowl, five times that distance.

Related: [[Cepheid variable]] (Polaris is the prototype of its class).
