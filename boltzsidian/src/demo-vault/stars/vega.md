# Vega

The fifth-brightest star in the sky, and the brightest in [[Lyra]]. At
magnitude 0.03 it is effectively the zero-point of the apparent
magnitude system — for a long time, photometry was calibrated by pointing
at Vega.

Vega has a debris disc, first detected in infrared by the IRAS satellite
in 1983. That disc is the reason I pay attention to it: a main-sequence
A-type star with the raw material of planets still visibly settling
around it, only 25 light-years away.

Vega is also the [[Precession|pole star of the future]] — in about
twelve thousand years the Earth's axis will wobble around to point
toward it, and Polaris will stop being useful.

Related: [[Altair]], [[Deneb]] — the rest of the Summer Triangle.
