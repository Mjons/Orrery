# Aquila

The eagle of the summer sky. [[Altair]] at its heart, flanked on
either side by Tarazed and Alshain — the three-in-a-row pattern that
makes Aquila instantly recognisable once you know to look for it.

Aquila rides the Milky Way between [[Cygnus]] to the north and
Sagittarius to the south. In a dark sky the summer band is visibly
dusty between them, the shadow of the [[Black hole|Sagittarius spiral
arm]] obscuring the central bulge.

Good binocular sweeping here — a lot of faint open clusters and
emission nebulae that don't have Messier numbers and so don't get
attention.

Related: [[Summer Triangle]].
