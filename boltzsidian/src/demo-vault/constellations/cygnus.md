# Cygnus

The swan flying south along the Milky Way. [[Deneb]] at the tail,
Albireo at the beak. The neck runs through one of the richest
stretches of galactic plane visible from northern latitudes.

Albireo is the binocular darling — a topaz primary and a sapphire
secondary, barely an arc-minute apart. The colour contrast is the
whole point; photos don't capture it the way a scope does.

Cygnus X-1, the first confirmed [[Black hole|black hole]] candidate,
lives here (invisible to the eye but famous by reputation). And the
North America Nebula spans an area larger than the moon, right next
to Deneb — a wide-field target best hunted with low-power binoculars
and an H-α filter.
