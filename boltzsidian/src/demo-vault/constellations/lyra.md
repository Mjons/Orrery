# Lyra

A small summer constellation dominated by [[Vega]]. Below Vega, four
dimmer stars form a slightly lopsided parallelogram — the frame of the
lyre.

Inside that parallelogram sits [[M57 Ring Nebula]], the planetary nebula
every amateur points at first. Even through bad seeing it reads as a
grey doughnut at low power.

Epsilon Lyrae — the "Double Double" — is one of my favourite party
tricks. In binoculars it's two stars. In the 8" at 200×, each of
those two splits into two again. A binary system of binaries.

Related: [[Deep sky challenge]], [[Summer Triangle]].
