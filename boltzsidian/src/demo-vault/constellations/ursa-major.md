# Ursa Major

The bear whose tail is a saucepan. The seven-star asterism we call the
Big Dipper anchors the northern sky — it's visible every clear night
from mid-northern latitudes, turning around the pole.

The two outer bowl stars, Dubhe and Merak, point at [[Polaris]]. Follow
the curve of the handle outward and you "arc to [[Arcturus]], speed on
to Spica." I can't set up the scope without running that memory.

Five of the seven stars share proper motion — they're part of the Ursa
Major Moving Group, a loose cluster of sibling stars still drifting
through the galaxy together. Dubhe and Alkaid are the two that don't
belong.

Related: [[Precession]], [[M51 Whirlpool Galaxy]] (just off the handle end).
