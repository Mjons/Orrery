# Cassiopeia

The W in the north. Circumpolar from my yard, always on the opposite
side of [[Polaris]] from the [[Ursa Major|Big Dipper]] — if one is up,
the other is down.

Cassiopeia sits in a crowded section of the Milky Way, so the binocular
sweep through it is dense with open clusters. [[Messier]] catalogued
two of them here: M52 and M103. Worth a whole winter of slow panning.

Tycho's supernova of 1572, SN 1572, went off in Cassiopeia — it was the
event that broke the Aristotelian idea of an unchanging celestial sphere.
