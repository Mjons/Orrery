# Photograph globulars

Long-term project: capture every [[Messier]] globular cluster accessible
from northern latitudes. Short exposures are fine — globulars don't need
the integration time a faint galaxy does — but each one deserves a real
session, not a snapshot.

**Why globulars?** They're photogenic, compact, resolvable with
modest aperture, and there's a finite list to complete. Unlike
galaxies they don't ask for a dark sky to be satisfying.

**Target list (northern-accessible):**

- [[M13 Hercules Cluster|M13]] — done, needs a better stack.
- M92 in Hercules — nearby, easier transparency demand.
- M3 in Canes Venatici — spring.
- M15 in Pegasus — autumn.
- M5, M10, M12 — all summer.
- M79 — winter, low altitude, hard from here.

Related: [[Deep sky challenge]], [[Telescope]].
