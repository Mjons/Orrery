# SALIENCE.md — The Sheath Around the Universe

A speculative design doc. BOLTZMANN.md proposes that dream-born ideas
get scored by a "meaning filter" — a function that decides which
children of the night survive to morning. This doc pushes that idea
one step further: instead of a filter (a gate you pass or fail), the
**salience layer** is a _spatial volume_ that surrounds the universe,
and an idea's score determines how it moves through that volume.

Good ideas rise. Unengaged ideas sink. Bad ideas dissolve. The user
sees what broke the surface.

Nothing here is a plan. It's a physicalisation of the salience concept
that might earn its keep as the Phase 6 implementation, or might
collapse back into a flat filter once built. Writing it down to find
out which.

---

## 0. Premise

A filter is a binary. `M > θ` → promote; `M ≤ θ` → discard. Binary is
honest but opaque: the user gets handed three ideas in the morning with
no sense of what almost-but-not-quite made it, what's still cooking,
or what the system considered and rejected.

A layer is a gradient. Ideas have **position** within it, **motion**
through it, and **a surface** where they become visible. An idea that
scores well but hasn't arrived yet can be _seen arriving_. An idea that
surfaced three days ago and still hasn't been opened is _seen sinking_.
The system's deliberation becomes a thing you can look at, not just an
opaque function return.

> The salience layer is what the app is doing while you're not looking.
> The morning report is a photograph of its surface.

---

## 1. What the sheath actually is

A soft volumetric shell around the field of note-bodies. Not a separate
scene — the same scene, with a new type of particle: the **mote**.

Each mote is one candidate idea produced by the dream cycle
(BOLTZMANN §5.2). At birth it lives at the interaction midpoint of its
two parent notes and has:

```
{
  id,
  seed_text,       // generated by the utterance backend
  affinity_vec,    // 8 floats, mix of parents
  parents: [a, b], // parent note ids
  score,           // S = novelty × coherence × reach × (1 - age)
  depth,           // 0 at surface, 1 deep in the sheath, dissolves at > 1
  touched_at,      // last user interaction (null until user sees it)
}
```

Motes are rendered dim and small. They glow just enough to be visible
in peripheral vision when ambient lighting allows, and fade out when
the user is reading a note or actively manipulating the scene (the
sheath is background, not foreground).

The sheath's shape follows the field — a rough convex hull of the
active cluster, offset outward by some radius. In a two-cluster demo
vault (Boltzsidian + Panel Haus) the sheath is roughly two connected
blobs, each with its own surface; motes born inside one can rise
through that cluster's surface or, if their affinity spans both, can
drift toward a shared surface.

---

## 2. What rises, and why

At each physics step, every mote in the sheath is pulled by a
**buoyancy force** proportional to `score - θ_neutral`:

```
F_buoyancy = k × (score - θ_neutral) × up_from_centroid
```

- Score above neutral: net upward motion. The higher the score, the
  faster the rise.
- Score at neutral: mote hovers in place.
- Score below neutral: net downward motion. Sinks.

`up_from_centroid` is the normalised vector from the nearest cluster's
centroid to the mote — so "up" means "outward to the surface," not a
global up. This keeps the geometry honest across cluster shapes.

The score is **not static**. It recomputes on every substantial vault
event — a new dream cycle spawning nearby motes, the user opening one
of the parent notes, another mote reaching the surface. Novelty
specifically can drop when a similar mote surfaces — the first mote
across was novel, the fifth isn't.

Because score can change, motes can reverse direction. An idea that
was rising on day one can sink on day three if the user opens similar
ideas in the interim. This is the system forgetting things that didn't
land — the same pattern as DREAM.md §3.3 pruning, at the idea layer
rather than the note layer.

---

## 3. The surface

The outermost radius of the sheath is the **surface**. Motes breach
it when their buoyant rise carries them past it.

On breach:

1. Visual: mote brightens, halo expands, it becomes clickable
   (pointerevents flip on). Shader-wise, the breach is a one-shot
   outward pulse that expands over ~1 s.
2. Log: the mote is recorded in the current dream's artifact bundle
   (feeds the morning report).
3. HUD: a single peripheral cue — the "noticed bell" in the top-right
   corner blinks once. The corner badge shows a small count of surfaced
   motes that haven't been opened yet.
4. Residence: the mote now floats _just outside_ the surface, held by
   a weak tether to its sheath point-of-breach, drifting slowly.

Surfacing is **the event** — but surfacing does not mean "keep this."
It means "this stood out enough to bring into view." What happens next
depends on the user.

---

## 4. The residence / dissolve loop

A surfaced mote sits at the edge of visibility. The user can:

- **Click it** — opens the mote's seed text in the ideas drawer. The
  mote is now _touched_; it stays surfaced indefinitely, pending an
  explicit decision. The peripheral glow softens (it has been seen).
- **Promote it** — the mote leaves the sheath, graduates into the main
  field as a real note-body. A file gets written to `ideas/…md`. The
  mote's halo fades over a few seconds; it is now a regular citizen.
- **Discard it** — mote dissolves immediately. No file. No trace.
- **Ignore it** — the _most common path_. If a surfaced mote hasn't
  been touched in `surface_dwell_s` seconds of wake-time (try 10
  minutes of active scene time, not wall-clock), it loses buoyancy and
  starts sinking back. If it reaches `depth = 1` without being touched
  again, it dissolves silently.

This is the bit DREAM.md §7 flags as load-bearing: **tune pruning to
hurt a little**. Ideas the user never engages with should visibly sink
and go away. Without that, the sheath fills, the surface gets crowded,
and every idea reads as equally noisy — the exact failure mode of a
Twitter notification drawer.

---

## 5. What the user sees

Normal day:

- The sheath is invisible. Zero motes.
- The user reads and writes notes as usual.

After a dream cycle (overnight or Dream Now):

- Maybe 20-40 motes were born in the sheath.
- Most sank and dissolved in the first sleep cycle. The filter did
  its job before the user even saw anything.
- Maybe 2-5 motes are floating near or at the surface.
- The peripheral noticed-bell in the corner shows a count.
- Morning report lists the surfaced ones in the "Three things"
  section — capped at three, even if more surfaced, because the
  promise (WORKSPACE.md) is three things, not twelve.

Over a week:

- Ideas surface, get ignored, sink, dissolve. Most days: zero to one
  promotion.
- The occasional idea that the user promotes leaves the sheath and
  joins the field — where it can become a parent for future dreams.
  (Yes: grandchild motes. This is where the recursive loop earns its
  keep.)
- The surface is almost always quiet. The sheath does work constantly
  but the user only sees the thin margin where it matters.

This is the aesthetic target: **a mostly-empty edge, occasionally
briefly populated, almost always fading.**

## 6. How it composes with existing layers

| Layer                        | What it does                        | Relationship to salience                                                                    |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| BOLTZMANN §2 observer chorus | Narrates the field in-scene         | Chorus can narrate the _sheath_ too — an idea rising at the edge is commentable             |
| BOLTZMANN §5.2 collisions    | Spawns candidate motes during dream | Produces the input to the sheath                                                            |
| DREAM §3.3 pruning           | Removes orphaned notes              | Same verb, different target (notes vs. motes)                                               |
| FORMATIONS §4                | Filter lenses on the universe       | A formation could include motes — e.g. "show me all rising motes above `θ_attention`"       |
| Phase 5 morning report       | Surfaces dream artifacts            | Now sources "Three things" from **surfaced motes** instead of chorus captions where present |
| K matrix                     | Learns which kinds cluster          | Weights the **reach** axis of score                                                         |

The sheath is a place, not a pipeline. Every previous layer feeds it
or reads from it.

## 7. Parameters we'd want to play with

All tunable from a dev palette; most also exposed to the user in
Settings under "Salience" once defaults are found.

| Param              | Default guess | What it does                                                         |
| ------------------ | ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `θ_neutral`        | 0.45          | Score below this and the mote sinks                                  |
| `θ_surface`        | 0.7           | Effective threshold for reaching the surface — smooth, via buoyancy  |
| `k_buoyancy`       | 8.0           | How aggressively score translates to vertical speed                  |
| `surface_dwell_s`  | 600           | Wake-seconds a surfaced mote can linger without touch before sinking |
| `dissolve_depth`   | 1.2           | Depth beyond which a mote evaporates                                 |
| `novelty_weight`   | 1.0           | Score multiplier for affinity distance from prior ideas              |
| `coherence_weight` | 0.8           | Score multiplier for parent-on-line coherence                        |
| `reach_weight`     | 1.2           | Score multiplier for kind-diversity in neighbours                    |
| `age_halflife_s`   | 86400         | How fast unreinforced score decays (1 day default)                   |

The four weights together define "what the system finds salient." A
user who tunes `novelty_weight` high will get a sheath that rewards
surprise. One who tunes `reach_weight` high will get a sheath that
rewards cross-domain ideas. The defaults should produce the behaviour
the exit gate (BUILD_PLAN §Phase 6) measures: 3-5 promotions across 10
dream runs on the demo vault.

## 8. Risks (or: failure modes to watch for)

### 8.1 The sheath is theatre

Risk: we put a glowing fog around the scene, nothing actually rises,
but the fog _looks_ like something is happening. Users read meaning
into the fog itself — "the universe is thinking."

Guard: the sheath is invisible when empty. No ambient glow, no mystery
fog. It appears only when there are motes in it, and even then only
brightens near motes, not uniformly.

### 8.2 Surface crowding

Risk: thresholds too low, surface gets 20 motes per dream, user
learns to ignore the corner bell, it becomes a badge-counter.

Guard: hard cap on surface residence (say 8 motes at once at the
surface). If a new one breaches while 8 are already there, the oldest
_unengaged_ one is bumped — it sinks first. "Three things" exists to
force concentration.

### 8.3 Confidence theatre

Risk: a user sees a mote at the surface and reads "the system has
decided this is important." Same Barnum effect the meaning-filter name
was carrying.

Guard: the language in the drawer and morning report is empirical, not
judgmental. "Noticed: idea surfaced at 03:14, depth 0." Never "the
app thinks this matters." Copy polices this more than UI does.

### 8.4 Sink shaming

Risk: user sees an idea they care about sinking, feels bad, promotes
it defensively → the system rewards that → they promote more
defensively → the vault fills with half-considered ideas. Engagement
as guilt.

Guard: sinking is silent and peripheral. No count of "ideas about to
dissolve." No "last chance to save" toasts. Ideas that dissolve
dissolve. If the user wanted to keep it, they would have touched it.

### 8.5 Hidden-work opacity

Risk: the sheath does everything invisibly and the user never learns
to trust it. Feels like a black box.

Guard: a dev-only view mode (`Shift+S`?) that renders the full sheath
with every live mote visible, their depths, their scores. Not for
daily use, but available as a way to understand what the system is
doing when something feels off. Like `git log` for the dream.

## 9. Minimal first cut

Shippable at the back end of Phase 6:

1. **Mote particles** — a second `THREE.Points` mesh with its own
   shader, additive blend, mote attributes (score, depth, parents). Up
   to 256 motes live at once (enforced by dissolve-oldest-if-over).
2. **Buoyancy force** — added to physics.step but gated on `bodyKind ===
MOTE`; runs only while dream depth > 0.15 or a mote is at the surface.
3. **Surface detection** — per-mote radial test against sheath radius
   each physics tick. On breach: fire the one-shot pulse, push into the
   "surfaced" list, add to dream artifacts.
4. **Residence list + decay** — surfaced motes have a `dwell` timer.
   When the timer expires without a touch, buoyancy flips to negative
   and the mote sinks.
5. **Ideas drawer** — existing Phase 6 deliverable, consumes the
   surfaced list. Adds click-to-open flow, promote / discard / ignore
   buttons.
6. **No dev view in v1**. Ship the user-facing edge first; the debug
   sheath comes after we've watched a few real weeks of use.

No persistence of mid-rise motes across reloads in v1. A mote either
makes it to the surface within the cycle it was born in, or it doesn't
get a second chance from that dream. This simplifies a lot and matches
how human sleep actually works — most night-thoughts don't get
morning encores.

---

## 10. What this is in one sentence

Ideas that want your attention rise to the surface of a shell around
your notebook; you can pick them up, let them sit, or watch them sink
back into the thing that produced them.

Flat tone, same as every other doc in this stack. Say "noticed," not
"realised." Say "surfaced," not "chosen." The layer does not know what
matters. It knows what stood out.

That's enough, if we get the thresholds right.
